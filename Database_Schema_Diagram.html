Reading the relations. Lines are foreign keys: a solid line runs from the table holding the FK to the table it references. Dashed lines are self-references (folders.parent_folder_id, comments.parent_comment_id). The two hubs — users and documents — collect the most lines because almost every governance row records "who did it" and "to which doc." The dynamic-approval chain is its own cluster: approval_policies → approval_policy_steps defines a chain, and versions → approval_step_events records walking it. None of the pre-existing tables gain an outgoing reference to the new ones — only documents.approval_policy_id (nullable) points in — which is why approval layering touches nothing else.
